Loan Information

The Veterans Affairs helps Veterans, Servicemembers, and eligible surviving spouses become homeowners. As part of our mission to serve you, we provide a home loan guarantee benefit and other housing-related programs to help you buy, build, repair, retain, or adapt a home for your own personal occupancy.

VA Home Loans are provided by private lenders, such as banks and mortgage companies.

VA guarantees a portion of the loan, enabling the lender to provide you with more favorable terms.

Information about VA loans to support veterans in homeownership.
  • No Down Payment: One of the primary benefits of VA loans is that they typically require no down payment. This can be a significant advantage for eligible borrowers who may not have substantial savings for a down payment.
  • Competitive Interest Rates: VA loans often have competitive interest rates. While the rates can vary, they are usually lower than those offered with conventional loans.
  • No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI): VA loans do not require private mortgage insurance (PMI), which is typically required for conventional loans when borrowers make a down payment of less than 20%.
  • Flexible Credit Requirements: VA loans tend to have more flexible credit score requirements compared to some other loan programs, making it easier for veterans and active-duty service members to qualify.
  • Funding Fee: Borrowers may be required to pay a one-time VA funding fee, which is a percentage of the loan amount. The amount of the funding fee can vary based on factors such as military service history, down payment amount, and whether it's a first-time or subsequent use of the VA loan benefit. In some cases, this fee can be waived for disabled veterans.
  • Loan Limits: VA loans do have loan limits, which vary by location and are adjusted annually. In some high-cost areas, the loan limits may be higher. Borrowers can still use a VA loan for amounts exceeding these limits, but they may need to make a down payment on the amount that exceeds the limit.
  • No Prepayment Penalties: VA loans typically do not have prepayment penalties, so you can pay off your loan early without incurring additional fees.
  • Refinancing Options: VA loans offer options for refinancing, including the Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL) and cash-out refinancing, which can be used to access the equity in your home.
  • Property Requirements: The property you intend to purchase with a VA loan must meet certain minimum property requirements, such as being in good condition and safe for occupancy.

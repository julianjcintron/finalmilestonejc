Our History

In the Fall of 2023, I was inspired to create a community supporting military individuals in their academic pursuits. Fueled by a desire to ensure our veterans thrive in college, I embarked on a journey to connect them with the resources they need.

In addition, I am working on building an online platform and establishing strategic partnerships, my goal is to provide a supportive network that empowers military personnel to excel in their academic endeavors. As I progress on this mission, I invite like-minded individuals to join me in making a meaningful impact on the educational journey of our veterans.

Student Veterans of America Chapter (SVA)
About
Resources
The UNH Manchester Student Veterans of America Chapter’s mission is to establish a veteran friendly environment, as well as promote UNH to veterans who are looking to further their education. Student Veterans of America (SVA) Chapter will assist veterans in taking full advantage of their education benefits and will lend support to local veteran organizations.
The SVA Chapter provides student military veterans, active-duty military members, and Guardsmen an organization for support and information pertaining to the Veteran Administration and the University of New Hampshire. In addition to Veteran VA education and medical benefits information, the Student Veterans of America Chapter would also provide a stress free and relaxed environment for its members to connect with military peers of UNH.
Furthermore, the Student Veterans of America Chapter will build exceptional relationships within the university and community, as well as team building atmosphere for peer support and advising.

How can I verify enrollment?

  • You can verify your enrollment via text message or by calling the Education Call Center (ECC) 1-888-GIBILL-1 (1-888-442-4551).
  • The ECC can also help you opt into text verification.
  • VA strongly recommends verifying enrollment via text.
  • If adding or dropping courses, make sure to reach out to a certifying official to keep your BAH on schedule.

Federal Pell Grant for Iraq & Afghanistan Service

  • If your parent or guardian died because of military service in Iraq or Afghanistan after the events of 9/11, you may be eligible for additional aid.
  • To be eligible, at the time of your parent’s or guardian’s death, you must have been less than 24 years old or enrolled at least part-time at a college or career school.
  • Payments will be adjusted if you are enrolled less than full-time.

Federal Pell Grants

  • If you meet the requirements above and are eligible to receive a Pell Grant, you will receive an Expected Family Contribution (EFC) of zero, which maximizes your Pell Grant eligibility and can increase your eligibility for other federal student aid programs.

Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grants

  • If you meet the requirements above but are not eligible for a Pell Grant based on your EFC, you will be eligible to receive the Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant.
  • The maximum amount of the Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant is the same as the maximum Pell Grant award.
  • Your EFC will not be affected, and therefore neither will your eligibility for any need-based federal student aid.

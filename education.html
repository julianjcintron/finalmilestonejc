Checklist and Resources

Here is the To Do List for Service Members attending UNH Manchester:

  • Complete the Application Process: Visit the Admissions Office at 88 Commercial Street, Manchester, NH, 03101 or apply online at manchester.unh.edu.
  • Submit FAFSA: Complete the Free Application for Student Aid (FAFSA) online at fafsa.ed.gov.
  • Apply for GI Bill®: Visit gibill.va.gov and select "Apply for Benefits".
  • Provide Certificate of Eligibility: Submit a copy of your Certificate of Eligibility to the UNH Manchester Student Accounts Office at 88 Commercial Street.
  • Submit Enrollment Certification Request: Complete the UNH VETERANS' Enrollment Certification Request form available at manchester.unh.edu/student/veterans-services. Submit the form up to 4 months prior to the first day of the semester and every semester you intend to use benefits.
  • Attend On-campus Orientation: Call 603-641-4170 to register for an on-campus orientation.
  • Schedule a Meeting with an Advisor: Register for your classes and meet with Jennifer in the Student Accounts office for certification.

For more information and support, contact the UNH Manchester Veterans' Services.

Quick Facts of UNH Campus - Manchester, NH
Campus
  • Established: 1985
  • Location: Heart of the state’s biggest city (110,000 residents)
  • Building: Pandora Building, a renovated mill in the city’s historic Mill Yard
  • Distance: 45 minutes from UNH’s main campus in Durham, one hour to Boston, 20 minutes to Concord
  • College & Schools: One of UNH’s 11 colleges & schools
Financial Aid
  • Percentage: 90% of students receive financial aid
  • Scholarships: $1 million in scholarships awarded annually
Student Life
  • Number of Students: 1,000+ undergraduate and graduate students
  • Demographics: 96% of students from New Hampshire
  • Clubs and Organizations: Variety of student-run clubs and organizations
  • Events: Many social and academic student events
  • Housing: UNH Housing close to campus and steps from downtown
  • Discounts: Student discounts at over 50 local retail stores, restaurants, ski resorts, theatres, museums, and more
Academics
  • Areas of Study: 50+
  • Degrees: Bachelor’s and graduate degrees offered
  • Focus: Experience-driven with a focus on emerging fields
  • Student to Faculty Ratio: 10:1
  • Faculty/Staff: 120+
  • Class Size: 98% of classes have fewer than 30 students
  • Library Resources: 2.8 million print and electronic resources in the library
  • Study Abroad: 570+ Study Abroad programs available
  • Millyard Scholars Program: Connects biotechnology, engineering technology, biological sciences, analytics and data science, computer science, and computer information systems students with scholarships and research opportunities
  • Labs and Studios: 16+ cutting-edge labs and studios
